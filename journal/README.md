# Daily Journal

One entry per class day, written **in your own words — no LLM for this**. The lessons train your hands; this trains your judgment, and it only works if it's actually you.

## How it works

1. At the **start** of each class day, copy `TEMPLATE.md` to a new file named with the date (e.g. `2026-06-11.md`) and fill in your goals.
2. At the **end** of the day, fill in the other three sections: what you learned, what was hard, what surprised you.
3. Commit it with your day's work.

## Why

- Writing down goals *before* you start is how you notice the difference between what you planned and what the LLM dragged you into.
- "What was hard" and "what surprised me" are the two most useful signals for planning future classes — be honest, not impressive.
- Six months from now, this folder will be the best record of how far you've come.

A few honest sentences per section beat polished paragraphs. If nothing was hard, you didn't push hard enough — say that too.
