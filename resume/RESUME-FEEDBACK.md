## 1. Fix today — privacy 🔒

Your full street address **and date of birth** are in this resume, and this resume is in a **public GitHub repo**. Anyone on the internet can read it.

- Remove the DOB entirely — it never goes on a US resume, anywhere.
- Replace the street address with "League City, TX." City and state is all a resume needs.
- Drop the "Personal Details" section completely ("Nationality: US Citizen" can be a single line *if* an application requires it — most don't).

## 2. The errors that cost you credibility

These are small, but they're in the worst possible places:

- **"Phyton"** → Python. This typo is in your *Relevant Skills* section — the one line that has to be perfect for someone interested in AI/ML.
- "Adobe Premier Pro" → Premiere Pro. "Aftereffects" → After Effects. "Beginners speaking level" → Beginner.
- **The grade math:** "Class of 2027, 12th Grade student (current)" — Class of 2027 means you're a junior right now. A reader who catches this doubts every other date.
- **The GPA labels are swapped:** the 5.457/6.0 *is* your weighted GPA; the 3.7 is your unweighted. Label them correctly: "GPA: 3.7 unweighted / 5.457 of 6.0 weighted."
- Dashes and dates are inconsistent throughout ("2023-continue", "2024- continue", mixed `-` and `–`). Pick one format: `2023 – Present`.

## 3. The big restructure — surface your best material

- **Your BSL2 lab work is hiding** as a bullet under "Additional Skills." Growth media, culture plates, autoclaving, colony counting, data analysis — that's a **Research Experience** section with its own heading, near the top. It's your single most impressive credential and right now it's invisible.
- **The awards list is a data dump.** 24 entries going back to a 2018 spelling bee. Cut everything from middle school except at most one or two truly exceptional items. When the list is short, your UIL win and NASA HUNCH finalist actually get seen. (Also: FRC team membership is an *activity*, not an award — move it.)

## 4. The deletion pass (Skill #3 — what would you remove?)

- Hobbies: cut the section. (Especially "Video Games.")

## 6. The one-page rule

A high-school resume is **one page**. Yours is three. If everything above gets done — cut middle school, cut hobbies, cut the personal details, merge the duplicates — you'll land at one page naturally, and it will be a much stronger page.
